03

Concept of operations

Single end-to-end flight. Times are illustrative; real values depend on rail-system geometry and apogee target.

  1. T−72:00:00
    Tug parking-orbit phasing
    Tug holds in low-energy elliptical orbit. Trim burns synchronize apogee passage with predicted pod arrival.
    routine
  2. T+00:00:00
    Rail release
    Pod separates from rail at exit velocity. Cold-gas attitude only — no main propulsion fires.
    critical
  3. T+00:04:30
    Ballistic ascent
    Pod follows ballistic arc. Apogee at ~150 km. Beacon transmits state vector to Tug.
    routine
  4. T+00:06:45
    Apogee rendezvous
    Tug matches velocity within 0.5 m/s and closes the last 30 m on cold gas. Capture confirmed by latch sensors.
    critical
  5. T+00:24:00
    TLI burn
    Tug + pod stack burns ~1,000 m/s for trans-lunar injection. Burn windows align with monthly lunar phasing.
    critical
  6. T+00:34:00
    Pod separation
    Tug releases pod on lunar transfer trajectory. Tug flips, performs return burn, drops back to phasing orbit.
    routine
  7. T+72:00:00
    Lunar delivery
    Pod arrives at Moon. Free return, LLO insertion, or surface impact depending on payload class.
    routine
04

Orbital mechanics

Two constraints set the architecture: inclination matching and apogee-velocity rendezvous.

Inclination matching

The Tug must launch into an orbital plane that contains the rail's exit vector. The rail's latitude and azimuth fix the minimum achievable inclination of the rendezvous orbit.

cos(i) = sin(az) · cos(φ) i = inclination · az = launch azimuth · φ = site latitude

Apogee rendezvous

At apogee, both the Tug and the pod move slowest. The relative-velocity budget for capture is smallest there. Six steps; the Tug does the work.

  1. Park
    Tug in phasing orbit
  2. Phase
    Trim apogee timing
  3. Coast
    Toward apogee · pod ascends
  4. Match
    v rel < 0.5 m/s
  5. Capture
    Latches engaged
  6. TLI
    Stack burns for transfer

Risks & open questions

Where the architecture is most likely to fail review. Each item names the discipline that owns it.

  • R1

    Pod survives rail acceleration

    Rail-launch g-loading constrains payload class. Most spacecraft electronics are not rated for the relevant peak.

    Owner · Pod structures
  • R2

    Apogee timing precision

    Tug arrival window at apogee is small. Atmospheric drag on rising pod adds dispersion; phasing-burn budget must absorb it.

    Owner · GNC
  • R3

    Single-shot capture

    Pod has no main propulsion. If Tug misses, pod is unrecoverable. No abort-to-orbit option.

    Owner · Mission ops
  • R4

    Cryo boil-off

    Methalox loiter losses across phasing duration cut into TLI margin. Either active cooling or shorter phasing.

    Owner · Propulsion / TCS
  • R5

    Inclination from inland sites

    High-latitude rail sites limit accessible inclinations and complicate downrange safety. Equatorial siting is preferred but politically constrained.

    Owner · Mission design
  • R6

    Reuse cadence vs refit

    Economic case relies on ≥10 cycles. Inspection, refurb, and propellant logistics determine real reuse rate.

    Owner · Operations
