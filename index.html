3 · Inclination constraint

Hazel Green sits at 34.93°N. An equatorial Tug orbit costs 4,620 m/s of plane change — more than the entire ΔV budget.

ΔVplane = 2 · vorbit · sin(Δi/2) = 2 · 7,784 · sin(34.93°/2) ≈ 4,620 m/s

A pod launched due east from a 34.93° latitude rail enters an orbit inclined 34.93°. The Tug must live there too — anywhere else means burning a budget-killing plane-change.

4 · Phasing & rendezvous

The pod arrives at apogee moving at 8–19% of orbital velocity. The Tug matches that slow vx with a gentle RCS burn — sub-m/s closing.

  1. Tug parking orbit at ~35° inclination, perigee below pod apogee.
  2. Phasing burn aligns Tug apogee with predicted pod apogee. ΔV ~10–100 m/s
  3. Tug coasts to apogee. v ≈ 7.8 km/s circular or less.
  4. Tug matches pod vx. Closing rate < 0.5 m/s. ΔV ~5–50 m/s
  5. Berthing arm closes. Capture over ~30–120 s.
  6. TLI burn from combined orbit. ΔV ~900–1,100 m/s
