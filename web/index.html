What you're looking at

Geometry

The Manna pod launches on a ballistic suborbital arc from the BGKPJR rail at 34.93°N. The Tug is pre-positioned in a 35° inclined orbit so that its apogee coincides with the pod's apogee in both altitude and in-track position. No plane change is needed; the launch latitude and orbit inclination match.

The apogee insight

At apogee the pod's horizontal velocity is the smallest it will ever be on the trajectory. Per the v0.1 doc, the residual vx is roughly 8–19% of orbital velocity at that altitude — the pod is nearly stopped horizontally, not arriving at 4 km/s. The Tug matches that slow velocity with a small RCS burn (5–50 m/s).
